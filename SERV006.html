  • Pitchfork quite correctly called The Embassy ”the architects of some of the past decades’ best new sounds”. With its blending of acoustic and digital elelements into a light and dreamy sound, elegant melodics and subtle lyrics, Futile Crimes – Service’s first album release – is the landmark work that opened the Göteborg 00:s era and a new chapter in music.
    Despite its limited audience, it may be one of the most influential Swedish records of all time.

    The artwork by Ola and Artur Zarkowski continues the pop coloured heraldy graphics of Ola's club night Lolita, which – like The Embassy – combined local indie and house music.
    Don't miss Stefan Zachrisson's beautiful liner note.
